To those of you who wondered what I was doing with my computer anyway...
//Sasq
ABC 80 ('85)
I went to this basic-course at Medborgarskolan in '85 and that was my first real experience with computers and I got hooked instantly. The ABC-80 was a swedish computer created by Luxor - if you're interested there's an emulator here.
  • Masken-slalom
    A simple game where you have to steer a worm down a course. I don't remember exactly how much of this game I wrote since there where other people invovled, but I know I did a (remake :) of it on the C64 later on.
C64 Basic ('86-'89)
In '86 me and my brother got a C64 to share. I continued my BASIC-programming and did many small programs. None of them are still with us (*sob*) and I don't even remember most of them, but these are some:
  • Wilhelm Tell
    You controlled a crossbow (sprite) on the left hand of the screen and had to shoot an apple (also a sprite) from the head of a man (character-graphics) on the right side of the screen. Made together with some modestly computer-interested friends in my neighborhood.

  • Mister Register
    A small register program where you could register persons with adresses and phonenumbers - and even load & save the data to tape.

  • Dragon Warriors
    This I did a bit later, when I actually had a diskdrive. It was based on the RPG Dragon Warriors and it let you roll your own characters and I think I had some type of combat in there too... and the beginning of some actual gameplay, but I dont think I got that far.
C64 Assembler
Actually, I never really got around to program assembler on the 64 until just before I bought my Amiga. All I did then was a character-clear routine for Dragon Warriors mentioned above, and some other tests. I got back to it later though and made one actual program.
  • Playsid64
    A program for playing c64-music on the c64! Eh, ok I know it doesn't sound that special but it was my first and only project. It loaded the file of your choise, parsing and printing the song-data on the screen (where the loader and player also relocated itself to so it handled music from before the basic-start ($801 or $810 ?) and up to $ffff which I was pretty pleased with :)
Amiga Demos ('90-'93)
Summer of '90 was when I first started programming for real on the Amiga; In Assembler of course. At the time I had mostly been mail-swapping games (ah-hem) with people, but soon dropped that in favour of the wonderful world of copperlists & vectorbobs, first reading the assembler-articles in Datormagazin, then diving into more serious litterature.
  • Cyberpunks-demo
    A week before the Swedish demo-event of the year, The Horizon party, I started coding on a demo and almost in time I had the buggy, evil, ugly piece of assembler that was the first and only demo from Cyber Punks as me and two other guys called us then (allthough they had nothing to do with the demo and wasn't really into computers). Lucky for us, it didnt work well enough to be releasable at the party.
    Details
Here me and a few people from the group Cizeron got together to form the group Azure.
  • Azure-demo
    This I'm a bit more pleased with. It was really called Skull-demo since it started with a laughing skeleton (shamelessly ripped from Barbarian II) and featured a bouncing logo, a sine-scroll and some nice rasters, and also with some descent graphcis from Skutt and music by Radioactivator (I think we asked him if we could use the tune but I'm not sure :)
    Details

  • Inzine!
    New group-name, Puddle with a very modest attitude (as you may gather from the name :) Inzine! was released at a small Atari party in Uppsala and featured a really big sine-scroll and some other stuff
    Details

Amiga Cracks
Yes, I occationally found it interesting to dig around in other peoples code... then these things can easily happen :)
  • Falcon
    Simple removal of the password check in the executable.

  • Supremacy
    Also password check, but this game was trackloaded. I had to put code in the bootblock that inserted jumps to itself at several stages during the loading to finally be able to remove the password-check when it was in memory.

  • PolyEd
    Patched so it always thought it was registered.

  • Spot
    Same, but made it crash in certain situations. There was a more widespread crack of this program that had the exact same problem.

Amiga C & Other ('93-'97)
  • Wolfdemo
    One of the last things I made in Assembler for the Amiga was a Wolfenstein-like demo (or doom-like as most people referred to them). I actually had a pretty fast routine but the code got to messy and I found it very tricky to put objects into the engine...
    Details

  • odsys
    A graphic-system for 2D-games. Featured run-length encoded sprites, resource-file handling and floyd-steinberg dithering of graphics which made it possible to run the same binary in a window or fullscreen and on plain OCS Amigas, AGA or Graphic- card equipped ones. This was actually pretty serious stuff when I think of it...
    Details

TI92
When I heard of this monster calculator in the summer of '96 I went out and bought it instantly. It contains a 10MHz 68000 processor (just like the old Amiga!), a QWERTY-keyboard and a 240x128 pixel display. It wasn't possible to program it in Assembler at the time I bought it, but it was only a matter of time since all earlier Texas Instruments calculators had been hacked to allow it. When it happened I wrote (among others) these:
  • Vectordemo
    Bouncing vector sporting a pretty optimized bresenham-routine.

  • Scrolling Bullock
    Big picture of Sandra Bullock showing off grayscale graphics :)

  • Asm92
    An actual 68000-assembler. Never released since it wasn't complete (some supervisor opcodes wasn't implemented). Made to look like Asmone; prompt based (except it had no editor).

Linux ('97-)
